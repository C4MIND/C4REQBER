#### **Mechanism:**

1. **CRISPR-Cas9 Targeting of ARGs:**  
   - Phages are modified to carry CRISPR-Cas9 systems programmed to cleave specific resistance genes (e.g., *blaTEM* for β-lactamases, *mecA* for methicillin resistance).  
   - Upon infection, Cas9 induces double-strand breaks in ARGs, reducing resistance prevalence.  

2. **Sub-Lethal Antibiotic Synergy:**  
   - Co-administration of sub-inhibitory antibiotics (e.g., β-lactams) disrupts bacterial SOS repair mechanisms, increasing susceptibility to CRISPR-mediated gene disruption.  
   - Antibiotics act as "stressors," weakening bacterial defenses against phage infection and CRISPR interference.

   - **Bacterial Population (B):**  
     \[
     \frac{dB}{dt} = rB \left(1 - \frac{B}{K}\right) - \phi P B - \mu A B
     \]  
     where \( r \) = growth rate, \( K \) = carrying capacity, \( \phi \) = phage infection rate, \( P \) = phage population, \( \mu \) = antibiotic killing rate, \( A \) = antibiotic concentration.  

   - **Phage Population (P):**  
     \[
     \frac{dP}{dt} = \beta \phi P B - \delta P
     \]  
     where \( \beta \) = burst size, \( \delta \) = phage decay rate.  

   - **Resistance Gene Frequency (R):**  
     \[
     \frac{dR}{dt} = \sigma B R (1 - R) - \gamma P R
     \]  
     where \( \sigma \) = horizontal gene transfer rate, \( \gamma \) = CRISPR-mediated gene disruption rate.  

   - **Prediction:** The system reaches a new equilibrium where \( R \rightarrow 0 \) if \( \gamma > \sigma \).
