Conclusion: c4reqber operates at TRL 1–3. It helps move from "I have a problem" to "I have a structured hypothesis with mechanism, predictions, and literature list." Everything beyond that requires a human with a lab, data, or mathematical apparatus.

1. PhD Students and Early-Career Researchers (Year 1–3)

Problem: "I know I'm interested in X, but I don't understand what's already been done and where the gaps are."

What c4reqber provides:

  • Literature review in 2 minutes instead of 2 weeks — 33 sources: arXiv, PubMed, Semantic Scholar, OpenAlex, Europe PMC, Crossref, Zenodo, and more
  • Structured gaps with potential assessment
  • Formulation of 3–5 competing hypotheses with falsifiable predictions
  • List of control experiments (DoE design)

Honesty: This is a starting point, not a final review. Hypotheses require expert verification — LLMs can hallucinate mechanisms.

2. Interdisciplinary Researchers

Problem: "I'm a biologist, but my problem requires physics/math/computer science. I don't know which tools from neighboring disciplines are applicable."

What c4reqber provides:

  • TRIZ analysis: 40 inventive principles applied to your problem
  • Isomorphisms: "Here are 3 similar problems from chemistry, solved with methods you don't know as a biologist"
  • C4 navigation across 27 cognitive states — structures chaotic thinking
  • Transfer pipeline: automatic analogy search between domains

Honesty: Works best when disciplines are close (biochemistry → biophysics). Quality drops for distant bridges (quantum mechanics → sociology).

3. Engineers and R&D Managers

Problem: "We need to improve a product/process, but the team is stuck in a local optimum."

What c4reqber provides:

  • TRIZ contradictions: "Increase speed without increasing energy consumption"
  • Patent landscape analysis (USPTO, OpenReview, Google Patents)
  • Structured "paradoxes" with proposed solutions
  • Simulations: CFD (OpenFOAM, FEniCSx), molecular dynamics (GROMACS, LAMMPS), multi-physics (MuJoCo, PyBullet)

4. Psychologists and Social Scientists

Problem: "My field is hard to formalize. Can AI even help study human behavior, therapy, or social phenomena?"

What c4reqber provides:

  • Literature search across psychological databases: PubMed (psychiatry), ClinicalTrials.gov, OpenReview, Harvard Dataverse, Kaggle
  • Causal inference (DoWhy, EconML, gCastle): "Does X affect Y?" with confounder control — critical for A/B tests in therapy
  • Agent models (Mesa, SimPy): modeling group behavior, norm diffusion, herding effects
  • Contradiction mining: finding contradictory claims in literature
  • TRIZ for social systems: 40 principles applicable to organizational problems

Honesty: c4reqber does not diagnose patients and does not replace a therapist. In social sciences, quality depends on data quality — if your niche has few peer-reviewed articles, LLMs will hallucinate. But for literature review, hypothesis structuring, and causal modeling, it is a powerful tool.

5. Clinical Researchers / Medical Doctors

Problem: "Need to design an RCT, find biomarkers, understand drug mechanism of action."

What c4reqber provides:

  • PubMed + Europe PMC + ClinicalTrials.gov + STRING + ChEMBL + PubChem
  • Causal inference for observational data: "Which factor actually causes the side effect?"
  • Simulations: pharmacokinetics (COPASI, Tellurium), PBPK models
  • Network pharmacology: STRING + Cytoscape-compatible output

6. Ecologists, Climatologists, Geographers

Problem: "Complex multi-factor systems, long time series, uncertainty in models."

What c4reqber provides:

  • GBIF (biodiversity), NASA Earthdata (satellite), USGS (geology), CERN (physics)
  • Bayesian models (PyMC, Stan): uncertainty quantification
  • Agent models: ecosystem simulations (predator-prey, invasive species)
  • Simulations: WRF (weather), FEniCSx (fluid dynamics for ocean modeling)

7. Data Scientists and ML Researchers

Problem: "Feature engineering, model selection, interpretation, causal claims from correlation data."

What c4reqber provides:

  • Kaggle + UCI ML Repository + Harvard Dataverse: dataset search
  • Causal inference: "Is this feature actually causal or just correlated?"
  • AutoML: automatic model enumeration (autoresearch layer)
  • Bayesian models: uncertainty quantification, model comparison (BMA)
  • Simulations: synthetic data generation for edge cases

8. Formal Verifiers / Theorists

Problem: "I have an idea, but I want to check it for contradictions formally."

What c4reqber provides:

  • Auto-generation of formal specification from natural language (Lean4, Coq, Dafny, Z3, Hoare)
  • Iterative compilation error correction (LLM → compile → error → fix loop)
  • Export proofs to .lean, .v, .smt2
  • MathDetector: automatic categorization of mathematical statements

Honesty: Auto-generation from natural language is not yet fully implemented. Works best if you explicitly provide the theorem statement.

Who It Does NOT Help (and Why)

WhoWhy it won't help
Experimenters who need dataThe system does not run experiments. It will say "you need to measure X" but won't measure X for you
Ultra-niche researchersIf your niche has <100 papers in 10 years, LLMs aren't trained on this data. Hypotheses will be generic or hallucinated
Those expecting guaranteed publishable resultsLLM generation is a creative trigger, not peer-reviewed science
Those who don't want to thinkThe system requires critical evaluation of output. Blind trust = noise
Practicing clinicians (treating patients)c4reqber is a research tool, not a diagnostic system. Do not use for diagnosis or treatment without peer review

Summary

c4reqber is a research assistant at the level of "a very smart PhD student who read 200 papers over the weekend and proposed 5 hypotheses."

  • Does not replace: experiments, expertise, peer review, scientist intuition, therapeutic care
  • Accelerates: literature review, hypothesis formulation, structuring thinking, contradiction search, causal modeling, interdisciplinary transfer

If you expect c4reqber to "discover a new law of physics" — you will be disappointed.
If you expect c4reqber to "systematize your chaos of thoughts and point where to look next" — you will get exactly that.
